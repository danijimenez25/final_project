About Me

Daniella Jiménez

Jiménez, a Miami native, is a journalist and graduate student at UC Berkeley's Graduate School of Journalism. She reports for the Investigative Reporting Program in Berkeley, California. Recently, she was awarded the NICAR25 fellowship by the Investigative Reporters and Editors organization. Her work has appeared in USA TODAY, Mother Jones, The Washington Post, CNBC, and NBC4/Telemundo44 in Washington D.C.

While at AU, she served as president of the school’s chapter of the National Association of Hispanic Journalists, was a reporting fellow at the Investigative Reporting Workshop, and earned the Ron Sutton Visual Literacy Award and the American News Women's Club Scholarship. The proud daughter of first-generation Colombian immigrants, she was raised in Hialeah, Florida.
